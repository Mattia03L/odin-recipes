Lasagna

Lasagne alla bolognese

Just as it is for any other traditional recipe, there are multiple recipes and lots of variants to the lasagna recipe,

but the Italian Academy of Cuisine, after a thorough analysis, has finally officially registered at the Bologna's

Chamber of Commerce what we have reasons to believe is the original traditional recipe

Ingredients for six portions

  • Dough sheets
    • 400 grams of white flour
    • 2 eggs
    • 250 grams of spinaches
  • Besciamella
    • 100 grams of 00 flour
    • 100 grams of butter
    • 1 liter of fresh whole milk
    • Table salt
    • Nutmeg
  • 300 grams of coarsely minced beef
  • 150 grams of pork pancetta
  • 50 grams of carrots
  • 50 grams of celery
  • 50 grams of onions
  • 300 grams of tomato sauce or peeled (already boiled and diced) tomatoes
  • half a glass of dry white wine
  • half a glass of whole milk
  • broth
  • olive oil or butter
  • salt
  • pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the meat sauce (Ragù)
    1. Slice the pancetta into small cubes, then mince it and stir-fry it into a pan
    2. Add 3 tablespoons of oil or 50 grams of butter, then add all the celery, carrots and onions and let them dry gently
    3. Add the minced beef to the mix and sear it
    4. Add the wine and mix until it evaporates completely
    5. Add the tomato sauce and let it simmer slowly (at very low heat) for about two hours, adding some broth if necessary
    6. Near the end add the half a glass of milk to dampen the tomatoes' acidity and adjust the flavor with salt and pepper
  2. Prepare the dough sheets
    1. Boil and sieve the spinaches
    2. Knead the white flour with the eggs and the boiled and sieved spinaches
    3. Knead the dough into thin sheets and cut it into larges squares
    4. Boil the square sheets and let them dry out on a piece of cloth
    5. While waiting for them to dry prepare the besciamella
  3. Prepare the besciamella
    1. Melt the butter in a saucepan at low heat, add the sieved 00 flour and mix with a whisk
    2. This sauce is called a "Roux", let the roux cook until it turns golden
    3. At this point add the liter of milk, salt and nutmeg
  4. Butter up a baking tray
  5. Inside it place first a layer of dried dough sheets,then add a layer of tomato sauce and then a layer of besciamella
  6. Repeat until none of the three aforementioned ingredients is left
  7. Cover up the last layer with besciamella and some butter flakes
  8. Cook in the oven at 160° Celsius for about 30 minutes
  9. When the surface turns golden and the lasagne are ready to be served
