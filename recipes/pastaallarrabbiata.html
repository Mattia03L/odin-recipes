Pasta all'arrabbiata

Pasta can be cooked in so many ways, with so many different types of sauces and ingredients,

yet the internationally most popular ones are always the same and their preparation is often wrongly executed

This is one of the simplest and most popular variants in Italy of tomato sauce pasta, it's so simple it's nearly impossible to get wrong

Ingredients for 3-4 people

  • 320g of pasta (Penne rigate if available)
  • 380g of tomato sauce, or (boiled, peeled) tomatoes
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 3 small dried hot peppers
  • Extravirgin olive oil
  • Table salt

Steps

  1. If you're using the tomatoes, smash them up into a sauce using a fork
  2. Crush the dried hot peppers
  3. While you prepare the sauce you want to fill a pot with water, put a big pinch of salt in it, cover it with the lid and bring it to a boiling temperature
  4. The ideal water-salt-pasta ratio is 10 grams of salt for every liter of water, 1 liter of water for every 100 grams of pasta
  5. Put some oil into a saucepan, then add a single peeled garlic clove and the crushed hot peppers
  6. When the clove starts turning slightly golden, add the tomato sauce and mix a bit
  7. Put the lid on and when it starts to boil, turn the heat to low and let it cook at for about 6 minutes
  8. Open the lid, taste, add salt as needed, then cover up with the lid and let it cook for another 6 minutes at very low heat
  9. When the sauce is almost done put the pasta into the boiling salted water pot, follow the cooktime written on its packaging
  10. Remember to stir the pasta inside the water a couple times during the cooking process, especially early on as the pieces would otherwise stick to each other
  11. Remove the garlic from the saucepan
  12. When the pasta is done, drain the water and put the pasta directly into the saucepan
  13. Stir it up and add a bit of pasta water if the sauce looks a bit too dry, then turn off the heat
